# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Nimbus exists to return agency to individuals over their own data. The people who build it should feel that same sense of agency in this community — safe to contribute, safe to ask questions, and safe to disagree.

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in Nimbus a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

**We encourage:**

- Respectful, direct technical discussion
- Constructive feedback on ideas and code — not on people
- Acknowledging when you're wrong and learning from it
- Asking questions at any level of expertise
- Prioritising the user's sovereignty in all design discussions

**We do not tolerate:**

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks in any form
- Publishing private information (doxxing)
- Trolling, deliberate derailment, or bad-faith argumentation
- Dismissing contributors based on experience level
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other forum where Nimbus community members interact on project matters.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported by opening a private contact with a maintainer or by emailing the project maintainers directly (see the repository contact details). All reports will be reviewed promptly and confidentially.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban contributors whose behaviour is harmful.
